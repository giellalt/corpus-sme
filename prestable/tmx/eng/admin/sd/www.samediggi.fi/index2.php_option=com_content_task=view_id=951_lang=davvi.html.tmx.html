Greenland Eyes - filbmafestivála Sajosis 20.-24.11.2014 International Film festival Greenland eyes comes to Finland on its Nordic tour in November.
Riikkaidgaskasaš filbmafestivála Greenland Eyes boahtá Supmii Davviriikkaid turnees skábmamánus. The festival brings the colors, sounds and atmosphere of Greenland to Sajos on 20th-24th of November.
Oaidninláhkai leat oanehisfilmmat, animašuvnnat, dokumeanttat, musihkkavideot, historjjálaš potreahtat ja unihka guhkes filmmat nu ruonáeatnanlaš go olgoriikkalaš bagadalliin. The festival will showcase a range of shorts, animations, documentaries, music videos and unique feature length productions both from Greenlandic and foreign film directors.
Greenland Eyes lea máilmmi stuorámus Ruonáeatnamii gulavaš festivála. Greenland eyes is the biggest festival focusing on Greenland.
Dat buktá ovdan filmmaid, mat ovddastit sierra genreid ja sierra áigodagaid. Oktasaš filmmaide lea dat, ahte dat muitalit midjiide juoiddá dálá Ruonáeatnamis. It features films across all genres each film selected to add an individual insight into today ’ s Greenland.
Festivála lágiduvvo dál nuppe geardde. The festival is organized for the second time.
Festivála vuosttas jagi 2012 filmmain návddašuvvui Berliinis. Dán jage filmmaid mearri lea lassánan nu olu ja beroštupmi leamašan nu viiddis, ahte ovtta gávpoga sajis festivála johtá dál buot Davviriikkaid. The inaugural festival took place in Berlin in 2012 and this year the number of submitted films was so great, and the interest so overwhelming, that the 2014 festival is touring all the Nordic countries.
Borgemánus Ruonáeatnamis álgán festiválaturnee lea ovdal Suoma geargan juo Dánmárkui, Fearasulluide, Islándai ja Ruŧŧii. Suoma maŋŋá maŋimuš čájeheamit dollojit Norggas juovlamánu 18. beaivve. Beginning in Greenland on August the 28 th the festival then travels to Denmark, Faroe Islands, Iceland, Sweden and Finland, before finishing in Norway on December the 18 th.
Filmmat čájehuvvojit Sámekulturguovddáš Sajosa auditorios. The screenings takes place in the auditorium of Sámi Cultural Center Sajos.
Filmmaide lea eaŋgalsgielat teaksta. All the films have subtitles in English.
